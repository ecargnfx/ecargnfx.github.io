About the Project

I founded a software company to help product teams test demand for new product ideas. As a founder, I did everything from research, branding, strategy, to design, development and marketing. This product evolved through a few iterations. Here's a glimpse into my design process.

1. User Research

To achieve our product vision, I need to first understand how experimentation ties into a product team's workflow. I interview target users to gain insight on their mental models, environments, and workflow. When I build up enough trust, they often invite me to their offices for contextual interviews!

The session ends with a compilation of raw data and artifacts that illustrate their pain points and workflow. This data narrows down on my target users and gives me a better idea for how our product fits into their work. I use this as inspiration and create personas to kick off my design process.

2. Synthesize Data

Based on the research, user goals, and business goals, I start creating several design models to map out how our software can integrate with our users' mental model.

3. Communicate

I create storyboards and write up a project brief to make sure my team and I are going in the same direction.

4. Rapid Prototyping

After brainstorming the various approaches, I wireframe a few interface ideas and test the prototypes with users. First paper prototypes, then interactive web prototypes, depends on what I'm testing.

5. Iterate

Based on user observation and questions, I realize the next design challenge to solve for is easing users into running their first hypothesis-driven experiments. There is too much cognitive load on the user's part in creating an experiment. I redesign a new approach in which the software "does the thinking," leading to faster decision-making for the users.

6. Pivot

After another round of testing and observation, we realized that although we can optimize for faster experimentation, the user's usage was not aligned with our product vision, so we switched direction and simplified the product to allow user discovery of viable new product ideas. This product launched with great success, gaining 1000 paying customers within a month.

7. Further Design Explorations

Design is a continuous process. I continuously analyzed qualitative and quantitative data to decide what to build next. One of the consistent user requests was progress reporting; identifying which products were meeting the metrics to show stakeholders.
